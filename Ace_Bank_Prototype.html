📝 Design Rationale Notes

🌳 Savings Tree (Not Badges)

The growing tree metaphor replaces traditional points/badges. It uses intrinsic motivation — watching something grow over time — rather than extrinsic reward tokens. This directly addresses the brief's warning against shallow gamification.

🔥 Savings Streaks (Not Spending)

Streaks are only tied to savings behavior, never to spending or borrowing. This is a deliberate ethical design choice — responsible gamification means not creating pressure to spend more.

🤖 AI Escalation Logic

The AI chatbot handles routine nudges and insights. When it detects a high-stress financial situation (debt, anxiety), it proactively offers a handoff to a human financial coach. The user always controls this transition.

🛡️ Trust & Security

Banking is a regulated, high-trust category. Every screen includes subtle trust signals (security badges, biometric prompts, FDIC-insured labels). This prevents the app from feeling like "just a game."

⌚ Omnichannel: Smartwatch

We chose smartwatch nudges over AR/VR because they change point-of-sale behavior in real time. AR for checking balances is a gimmick; a timely wrist notification before a purchase genuinely impacts spending.

🧠 Behavioral Framework: Fogg Model

Every gamified element maps to Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. Challenges increase ability (micro-goals). Community provides motivation (social proof). AI nudges are the prompts.

🚫 What We Deliberately Left Out

No leaderboard for "biggest spender." No streak pressure on debt repayment. No AR/VR features. These omissions are as important as what we included — they demonstrate design restraint in a regulated industry.
