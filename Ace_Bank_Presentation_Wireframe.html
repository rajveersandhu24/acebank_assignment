4. Core Design Principles

The rules governing our gamification strategy

1. Progress Over Perfection

Reward consistency, not just magnitude. Break down large financial goals into micro-milestones.

2. Nudge, Don't Nag

Use AI to provide contextual, actionable insights at the right time, avoiding notification fatigue.

3. Responsible Gamification

Never use streaks to encourage overspending or debt. Gamify savings and education, not borrowing.

8. Design Rationale & Trade-offs

Defending the "Why" behind the design

Why we rejected basic badges & leaderboards:

They drive short-term extrinsic motivation but fail to build long-term financial health. Instead, we focused on intrinsic visual progress (e.g., growing a virtual tree tied to a savings account).

Why we limited AR/VR:

We chose to prioritize wearable nudges (smartwatches) over AR features. AR for checking balances is a gimmick; timely smartwatch nudges actually change point-of-sale behavior.

Ethical Stance:

We deliberately disabled "streaks" for debt repayment to avoid adding psychological pressure to vulnerable users, restricting gamification to positive wealth-building behaviors.

9. Next Steps & Validation

What we would test with users tomorrow

Hypotheses to Validate:

  • Tone of AI Nudges: Does the AI sound too casual or too parental? We need to test the conversational copy with Gen Z users to find the sweet spot of trust.
  • Streak Anxiety: Do savings streaks cause anxiety if broken? We need to test the "grace period" or "streak freeze" mechanic.
  • Omnichannel Handoff: Is the transition from the AI chatbot to a human financial coach seamless and reassuring?
